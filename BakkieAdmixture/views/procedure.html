Food Procedures

1. Biryani

  • Step 1: Wash and soak basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
  • Step 2: Heat water in a pot, add whole spices (bay leaf, cardamom, cloves, cinnamon), and cook the soaked rice until 70% done. Drain and set aside.
  • Step 3: In a pan, heat ghee or oil and sliced onions until golden brown. Remove half for garnishing later.
  • Step 4: Add ginger-garlic paste and sauce until fragrant.
  • Step 5: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  • Step 6: Add marinated meat or vegetables (marinate with yogurt, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt for at least an hour) and cook until tender.
  • Step 7: Add fresh coriander and mint leaves for enhanced flavor.
  • Step 8: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer half of the cooked rice over the spiced meat/vegetable mix.
  • Step 9: Sprinkle saffron-infused milk, fried onions, and a little ghee on top.
  • Step 10: Repeat the layering process with the remaining rice and garnishing.
  • Step 11: Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat (Dum cooking) for 20–25 minutes.
  • Step 12: Turn off the heat and let it rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid.
  • Step 13: Gently mix the biryani without breaking the rice grains.
  • Step 14: Garnish with boiled eggs, additional fried onions, and fresh herbs if desired.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with raita, salad, or a side of curry.

2. Sambar Rice

  • Step 1: Wash and soak 1 cup of rice and ½ cup of toor dal (pigeon pea lentils) for 20–30 minutes.
  • Step 2: In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dal and rice with 3 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a little salt. Cook for 3–4 whistles until soft.
  • Step 3: In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee and sauté mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves until they splutter.
  • Step 4: Add finely chopped onions and green chilies, sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Step 5: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
  • Step 6: Add chopped vegetables like carrots, beans, drumsticks, brinjal, and pumpkin.
  • Step 7: Pour 2 cups of water, add salt, and let the vegetables cook until they are tender.
  • Step 8: Add 2 tablespoons of sambar powder, mix well, and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  • Step 9: Add tamarind extract and stir well to balance the flavors.
  • Step 10: Mash the cooked rice and dal mixture slightly, then add it to the sambar.
  • Step 11: Stir everything together and let it simmer for 5 minutes until well combined.
  • Step 12: If needed, add water to adjust consistency and let it cook for another 2 minutes.
  • Step 13: Add a small piece of jaggery for a slight sweetness (optional) and mix well.
  • Step 14: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves and a dollop of ghee for extra flavor.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with papad, pickle, or curd for a delicious meal.

3. Vegetable Rice

  • Step 1: Wash and chop mixed vegetables like carrots, beans, capsicum, and cabbage into small pieces.
  • Step 2: Cook 1 cup of basmati rice with 2 cups of water until it's fluffy. Let it cool completely.
  • Step 3: Heat oil or butter in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Step 4: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Step 5: Add minced garlic and green chilies, and sauté until fragrant.
  • Step 6: Add chopped vegetables and stir-fry on high heat for 2-3 minutes.
  • Step 7: Add spices like turmeric, black pepper, and a pinch of garam masala for extra flavor.
  • Step 8: Add soy sauce and mix well to coat the vegetables evenly.
  • Step 9: Reduce the heat and add the cooked rice to the pan.
  • Step 10: Gently mix everything together without breaking the rice grains.
  • Step 11: Adjust salt and spices as needed, and let it cook for 2 more minutes.
  • Step 12: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and spring onions.
  • Step 13: Drizzle a little lemon juice for extra freshness (optional).
  • Step 14: Serve hot with raita, pickle, or a side of curry.

4. Sambar

  • Step 1: Wash and soak ½ cup of toor dal (pigeon pea lentils) for 20 minutes.
  • Step 2: In a pressure cooker, add the soaked dal, 2 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a little salt. Cook for 3-4 whistles until soft.
  • Step 3: Mash the cooked dal slightly and set it aside.
  • Step 4: Soak a lemon-sized ball of tamarind in warm water for 10 minutes. Extract the juice and discard the pulp.
  • Step 5: In a pan, heat oil and sauté mustard seeds, cumin seeds, dried red chilies, curry leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida.
  • Step 6: Add chopped onions and green chilies, and sauté until onions turn translucent.
  • Step 7: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft.
  • Step 8: Add vegetables like drumsticks, carrots, brinjal, and beans. Stir-fry for a minute.
  • Step 9: Pour in the tamarind juice, add salt, and let the vegetables cook until tender.
  • Step 10: Add 1-2 tablespoons of sambar powder and mix well.
  • Step 11: Stir in the cooked lentils and let the sambar simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  • Step 12: If needed, add water to adjust consistency and bring it to a gentle boil.
  • Step 13: Add a small piece of jaggery (optional) to balance the flavors.
  • Step 14: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with rice, idli, dosa, or vada.

5. Chana Masala

  • Step 1: Soak 1 cup of dried chickpeas in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  • Step 2: Drain the water and pressure cook the chickpeas with 3 cups of water and a pinch of salt for 4-5 whistles, until tender.
  • Step 3: In a pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Step 4: Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Step 5: Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Step 6: Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft and mushy.
  • Step 7: Add spices like turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and a pinch of salt. Mix well.
  • Step 8: Mash a few chickpeas to thicken the curry, then add the cooked chickpeas to the gravy.
  • Step 9: Pour 1 cup of water and let the curry simmer for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Step 10: Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed and simmer for another 5 minutes.
  • Step 11: Add a teaspoon of lemon juice for a tangy flavor.
  • Step 12: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander leaves.
  • Step 13: Turn off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes before serving.
  • Step 14: Serve hot with rice, naan, or chapati.
  • Step 15: Enjoy your homemade chickpea curry!

6. Samosa

  • Step 1: In a bowl, mix 2 cups of all-purpose flour, ½ teaspoon salt, and 2 tablespoons oil or ghee.
  • Step 2: Gradually add water and knead into a firm, smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  • Step 3: Boil 2 large potatoes, peel, and mash them in a bowl.
  • Step 4: Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds. Let them splutter.
  • Step 5: Add finely chopped green chilies, ginger, and peas. Sauté for a minute.
  • Step 6: Add mashed potatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Step 7: Stir in fresh coriander leaves and lemon juice for extra flavor. Let the filling cool.
  • Step 8: Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a thin oval shape.
  • Step 9: Cut each oval in half to form two semi-circles.
  • Step 10: Take one semi-circle, apply water along the edges, and fold into a cone shape.
  • Step 11: Fill the cone with the potato mixture and seal the edges tightly.
  • Step 12: Heat oil in a deep pan and fry the samosas on low-medium heat until golden brown.
  • Step 13: Remove from oil and drain on a paper towel.
  • Step 14: Serve hot with green chutney or tamarind chutney.
  • Step 15: Enjoy your crispy homemade samosas!

7. Gajar ka Halwa

  • Step 1: Wash, peel, and grate 4-5 medium-sized carrots.
  • Step 2: Heat 2 cups of full-fat milk in a heavy-bottomed pan and bring it to a boil.
  • Step 3: Add the grated carrots to the boiling milk and mix well.
  • Step 4: Cook on low-medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the carrots soften and absorb the milk.
  • Step 5: Once most of the milk is absorbed, add ½ cup of sugar and mix well.
  • Step 6: Continue to cook, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens.
  • Step 7: Add 2 tablespoons of ghee and stir well to enhance the flavor.
  • Step 8: Add ½ teaspoon of cardamom powder for aroma and mix.
  • Step 9: In a separate small pan, heat a teaspoon of ghee and fry chopped almonds, cashews, and raisins until golden.
  • Step 10: Add the fried nuts and raisins to the halwa and mix well.
  • Step 11: Continue cooking until the ghee separates and the halwa reaches the desired consistency.
  • Step 12: Turn off the heat and let it rest for a few minutes.
  • Step 13: Transfer to a serving bowl and garnish with extra nuts.
  • Step 14: Serve warm or chilled as per preference.
  • Step 15: Enjoy your delicious homemade Gajar Ka Halwa!

8. Pasta

  • Step 1: Boil water in a large pot, add salt, and cook 2 cups of pasta until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  • Step 2: Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté minced garlic until fragrant.
  • Step 3: Add finely chopped onions and cook until soft and translucent.
  • Step 4: Add chopped tomatoes or tomato puree and cook until the sauce thickens.
  • Step 5: Season with salt, black pepper, red chili flakes, and dried oregano.
  • Step 6: Stir in fresh basil leaves for an authentic Italian flavor.
  • Step 7: (Optional) Add a splash of cream for a rich, creamy texture.
  • Step 8: Add the drained pasta to the sauce and toss well to coat evenly.
  • Step 9: Let the pasta cook in the sauce for 2-3 minutes to absorb the flavors.
  • Step 10: Adjust seasoning and add a drizzle of olive oil for extra richness.
  • Step 11: (Optional) Mix in sautéed vegetables like bell peppers, mushrooms, or spinach for extra taste.
  • Step 12: Remove from heat and transfer to a serving plate.
  • Step 13: Sprinkle grated Parmesan or mozzarella cheese on top.
  • Step 14: Garnish with fresh basil or parsley.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with garlic bread on the side and enjoy!

9. Mushroom Gravy

  • Step 1: Clean and slice 2 cups of mushrooms.
  • Step 2: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil or butter in a pan.
  • Step 3: Add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  • Step 4: Sauté finely chopped onions until golden brown.
  • Step 5: Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Step 6: Stir in chopped tomatoes or tomato puree and cook until soft.
  • Step 7: Add spices like turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt.
  • Step 8: Cook the spice mixture until oil starts to separate from the sauce.
  • Step 9: Add sliced mushrooms and stir well to coat with the sauce.
  • Step 10: Pour in ½ cup of water or coconut milk for a creamy texture.
  • Step 11: Cover and cook on low heat for 5-7 minutes until mushrooms are tender.
  • Step 12: Stir in fresh cream (optional) and mix well.
  • Step 13: Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  • Step 14: Let it rest for a couple of minutes before serving.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with rice, naan, or roti.

10. Fish Curry

  • Step 1: Clean and cut the fish into pieces. Pat dry.
  • Step 2: Marinate the fish with ½ teaspoon turmeric powder, salt, and a little lemon juice. Set aside for 15-20 minutes.
  • Step 3: Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a pan.
  • Step 4: Add mustard seeds and let them splutter, then add curry leaves and sauté for a few seconds.
  • Step 5: Add finely chopped onions and cook until golden brown.
  • Step 6: Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and chopped green chilies. Sauté until fragrant.
  • Step 7: Stir in chopped tomatoes or tomato puree and cook until soft and mushy.
  • Step 8: Add spices like red chili powder, coriander powder, turmeric, and garam masala. Mix well.
  • Step 9: Pour in tamarind extract and stir well.
  • Step 10: Add 1 cup of coconut milk and mix until the curry comes to a gentle boil.
  • Step 11: Carefully add the marinated fish pieces to the curry.
  • Step 12: Cover and simmer on low heat for 10-15 minutes until the fish is fully cooked.
  • Step 13: Adjust salt and spices as needed, then turn off the heat.
  • Step 14: Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and a drizzle of coconut oil for extra flavor.
  • Step 15: Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
